    The betrayal of Nik Naks

    Lets start from the end. Nik Naks, the most popular snack in South Africa, how did this come to be? In the 2000’s the Nik Naks mascot was change due to concerns over the racially prejudicial nature of the original image featuring a stylized Kaapse Klopse minstrel. This mascot was a symbol of the tool for evil that Nik Naks were.
    This article discuss a lot about the racist history of Nik Naks, but it misses the real dirty that these seemingly innocent maze snacks covered up.
    Now lets go to the beginning. The Simba chip company has been around since 1957, and to still be dominating the snack market is no coincidence. It’s no surprise Simba had to make a deal with the devil to be where they are today.
    Nik Naks were released in 1972 after much development from Simba. And were pushed hard into the market.
    We need to understand the south African environment during this time, to try comprehend what exactly went down.
    Up until the 60’s the government had successfully managed to subdue all outspoken on the apartheid regime. In 1968 the government received intel on a growing group on black workers that had plans to protest and were massing up numbers quick. In response the government established The Bureau of State Security, also known as BOSS. BOSS was headed up by Hendrik van den Bergh, who’s as murky as the history of BOSS. The main aim of BOSS was to monitor national security, as well as to recognize any potential threats to the South African state.
    van den Bergh knew the government were outnumbered by the fast growing anti-apartheid movement and any violent attack would spin the country into chaos. If the situation was left, then the regime risked collapsing.
    This led to the partnership of the Simba Chips company and BOSS.
    BOSS incentivized Simba, with tax reductions to produce a new snack that could be pushed into areas of black workers and townships.
    Upon consumption of this snack, a toxin called Staphylococcus, that attacks white blood cells would start working on its consumer. The idea was to subdue someone who ingests the snack by weakening their immune system leading to them becoming sick more frequently and experiencing a loss of energy.
    The strategy worked for a long time, delaying the inevitable violence and protests by at least a decade.
    In the mid 1980’s, Simba pulled with BOSS when things started to not look good for the current government. Simba, changed their recipe for Nik Naks and covered almost all connection to BOSS, like nothing ever happened.
