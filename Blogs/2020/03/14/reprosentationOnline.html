A reflection on online representation


How does one represent themselves online? To answer this we need to understand what the online space is. Much like a game space, the internet ,and especially social media, provides an realm that is separate from that which we live.
Johan Huizinga coined the term ‘the magic circle’, the magic circle describes and area, physical or not, of play which was metaphorically speaking separate to our world. The rules in this circle were different to the rules we faced in everyday life, but there were still rules. Huizinga attempted to describe play, what it meant, and why it was so important.
By this, the internet in some way has elements of play. The rules online are not the same we conform to in everyday life. It is an escape from our ‘real’ life. We engage and interact with people much differently, often in a way that is very uncharacteristic of us (or maybe the true characteristic of us).
With technology becoming a larger and larger part of our everyday lives, we can see these circles colliding. Trends on the internet are being acted out physically. People often do things in reality just for the opportunity to add it to there online representation. The goals people have for there online lives are similar to those that they have for their ‘real’ lives.
Keeping this in mind, the way I present myself online, I think, is similar to my offline self. I’m much more of a consumer than an active poster. On sites where the engagement is primarily with people I engage with in ‘real’ life (such as social media), I am more reserved and closer to my offline self. I think this is because although the internet is its own magic circle, as it becomes more integrated into our lives, more of our offline personality will merge with our online personality. This includes our positive and negative traits, in my case social insecurities make me more of a consumer. On sites where I am more anonyms to people in my everyday life, such as reddit, I tend to be more riskier, and engage with more content.
We need to be mindful of our online personality, it can give one great insight into who they are or who they want to be. As these two magic circles of different worlds and rules collide, it will be tricky to separate the two personalities. It is our job to take bits from each one that we think are positive, to make one whole complete person, that can navigate both worlds properly.
