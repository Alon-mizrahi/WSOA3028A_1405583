The Implicit Bias Tests


Everybody has thoughts and feelings that they are not entirely aware of. These unconscious thoughts can be very subtle. We all posses certain leanings, mostly known to us, but sometimes they are not. These hidden biases affect the way we act and think, in small and large ways.
The Implicit Bias Tests(IBT), is a test which tries to figure out your subconscious leanings. An implicit bias refers to the attitudes or stereotypes that affect our understanding, actions, and decisions in an unconscious manner.
I will be talking about the my results from this Implicit Bias Test I took and what effect I think these implicit biases have on shaping the internet. Its important to note that the test is not perfect and outside factors can affect results. These results are only for educational purposes and interest.
In the IBT regarding age I scored a Strong automatic preference for Young compared to Old. These results suggest that I subconsciously very much prefer young people to old people. What’s interesting is that consciously I would say I have a moderate preference for Young compared to Old.
In the IBT regarding sex I scored a slight automatic preference for Straight people over Gay people. These results suggest that I subconsciously prefer straight people to gay people. Again consciously I would say I have no preference to either group.
The results, although personal, lead to an interesting conclusion; people have subconscious leanings to certain groups. These leanings can change the way we interact with groups, and in the case of the web, change the way we design it. For example, if a designer of a product was thinking more about a certain type of person using this product, then the product will be designed more for that certain person.
In terms of the internet, its clear to see that older people struggle to use and navigate the web like we do. Even though they weren’t born with it, most older people have had a few years of experience dealing with the web and still can’t seem to figure it out. This could be due to web designers subconsciously designing sites that cater to younger people, unintentionally excluding older people from the site.
As a potential future web developer it is essential to constantly be thinking about all types of people how would use the site and how to make it accessible to them all. This can be accomplished generally through thoughtful and careful UI and UX design.
